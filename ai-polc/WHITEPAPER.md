## The Problem

Product ownership is the most overloaded role in modern software delivery. The Product Owner is simultaneously expected to: manage a backlog, write user stories, prioritize features, define acceptance criteria, coordinate stakeholders, track benefits, manage releases, and somehow also have time to think strategically about what the product should become.

The typical pattern: a Product Owner maintains a flat backlog in Jira. Stories are written ad-hoc. Prioritization is gut feel or "whoever shouted loudest." Acceptance criteria are vague or missing. There's no traceability from business objectives to backlog items. Benefits are promised at project initiation but never tracked. Release scoping is "whatever's ready."

The result: backlogs become graveyards. Teams build features that don't trace to value. The PO becomes a ticket writer instead of a product strategist.

---

## The Solution

AI-POLC provides a structured lifecycle for product ownership — from defining value goals and personas through backlog creation, prioritization, acceptance, release planning, and benefits realization. It transforms the PO from a reactive ticket writer into a strategic product governor.

**From business objectives to governed backlog to tracked benefits.**

The AI acts as a disciplined Product Analyst / Agile Coach. It derives backlog items from architecture and business goals (not invents them), applies systematic prioritization models (WSJF, MoSCoW, value-based), and maintains traceability from business case through delivery to benefits realized.

## How It Works

```
PIP + AP → FOUNDATION → STRATEGY → OPERATIONS → GOVERNANCE → ASSEMBLY → Product Backlog Package
```

**Multi-phase lifecycle with continuous operations:**

| Phase | What Happens | Key Output |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Foundation | Define product vision, value goals, success metrics | Product Vision & Value Framework |
| Strategy | Define personas, map user journeys, establish prioritization model | Personas, Journey Maps, Prioritization Scorecard |
| Operations | Create backlog, write stories, define acceptance criteria, plan releases | Product Backlog, User Stories, Release Plan |
| Governance | Track velocity, manage stakeholders, review benefits | Dashboards, Stakeholder Reports, Benefits Log |
| Assembly | Package all product ownership artifacts for handoff and continuity | Product Backlog Package (PBP) |

AI-POLC runs **bidirectionally with AI-DLC v1** — it feeds stories into the build and receives delivery feedback back. It also exchanges with AI-UXD (personas/journeys inform UX research and vice versa).

## Key Differentiators

### 1. Value-Traced, Not Ad-Hoc

Every backlog item traces to a business objective or architectural commitment. Nothing exists "because someone asked for it" without connecting to measurable value. The traceability chain: Business Case → Value Goal → Epic → Story → Acceptance Criterion → Delivered Feature → Benefit Measured.

### 2. Bidirectional with Build

AI-POLC doesn't stop at "backlog created." It maintains a live feedback loop with AI-DLC v1 — stories flow into delivery, acceptance results flow back, and the PO is informed of progress, blockers, and scope risks in real time.

### 3. Systematic Prioritization

No more "gut feel" prioritization. AI-POLC applies WSJF (Weighted Shortest Job First), MoSCoW, or value-based models — configured per project. Every prioritization decision has a score, not just a position in the list.

### 4. Benefits Realization (Not Just Delivery)

Most agile tools track "did we build it?" AI-POLC extends to "did it deliver the value we promised?" Benefits realization tracking connects delivery back to the business case — closing the accountability loop.
