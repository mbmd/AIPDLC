## The Problem

Ideas are cheap. Good ideas are rare. And the mechanism to tell them apart is usually missing.

The typical pattern: someone has an idea — in a meeting, a Slack thread, a sticky note. It gets verbal approval ("sounds good, go ahead") and lands directly on a team's backlog. Three months later, nobody can recall why this project exists, what problem it was supposed to solve, or how it was evaluated against the other five ideas that were never written down.

The worst outcome isn't a bad idea getting built — it's that teams build the wrong thing because there's no structured way to evaluate ideas against each other. No evaluation criteria. No effort estimate. No alignment check. Just enthusiasm and proximity to a decision-maker.

---

## The Solution

AI-ILC provides a structured funnel for ideas before they become projects. It takes a raw idea — a sentence, a paragraph, a conversation — and walks it through shaping, evaluation, and routing. Every idea gets the same fair assessment. Every idea produces an artifact that explains why it was approved, deferred, or rejected.

**From "I have an idea" to "here's why we should (or shouldn't) pursue it."**

The AI acts as a disciplined innovation analyst. It doesn't judge enthusiasm — it evaluates feasibility, alignment, effort, and impact. Ideas that pass become Approved Idea Briefs, ready for AI-PILC or direct routing into the portfolio.

---

## How It Works

```
Raw Idea → CAPTURE → SHAPE → EVALUATE → ROUTE → Approved Brief / Rejected with Rationale
```

**4 Phases, structured flow:**

| Phase | What Happens | Key Output |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Capture | Receive the raw idea in any format (verbal, written, diagram) | Structured Idea Record |
| Shape | Expand: problem statement, target users, expected outcomes, constraints | Shaped Idea (evaluable form) |
| Evaluate | Score against defined criteria (alignment, feasibility, effort, impact, risk) | Evaluation Scorecard |
| Route | Decision: Approve → AI-PILC / Fast-track → AI-ADLC / Defer / Reject | Approved Idea Brief or Rejection Record |

Every stage has a gate. The user (idea owner or PMO) decides — the AI recommends.

## Key Differentiators

### 1. The Funnel Before the Funnel

AI-ILC sits before AI-PILC. Not every idea deserves a full project initiation process. AI-ILC is the triage nurse — routing ideas to the right level of investment: full project initiation, fast-track to architecture, defer for later, or reject with recorded rationale.

### 2. Evaluation, Not Opinion

Every idea is scored against the same criteria: strategic alignment, technical feasibility, estimated effort, expected impact, and risk. The scoring model is explicit and adjustable. No idea is rejected without evidence — and no idea is approved without it either.

### 3. Optional but Valuable

AI-ILC is the only package in the family marked optional. The chain works without it (start at AI-PILC). But organizations that use it get: visible demand pipeline, consistent evaluation, and audit trail for every investment decision.

### 4. Connectors for Context

AI-ILC includes connectors that pull context from existing tools — Jira epics, GitHub discussions, Confluence pages — so ideas aren't evaluated in a vacuum. The evaluator sees the full picture.

---

## Position in AIFLC — the AI-* PDLC Family

AI-ILC is the **optional entry point** to the Portfolio layer. It feeds two consumers:
- **AI-PILC** — Approved ideas that need full project initiation
- **AI-PPM** — Portfolio register (demand pipeline visibility)

Ideas that don't warrant a full PIP can be fast-tracked directly to AI-ADLC (for small architectural decisions) via AI-FLO routing.
